How Logo Branding Impact the Companies?




Logos are something which we have seen a lot in our daily life. We commonly have seen posters, billboards, video ads, newspapers, tv, and many other things and in those other than the product, we see some other elements like some shapes, pictures, or symbol. And that is called a logo.


But the question is why logos are used? What is their purpose? How these logo branding impact the respective companies? How can we make it? Today we will discuss all these things, and if some start-up aspirant is reading these, then buddy, read it carefully.



So, in starting, why are logos used?


Before that, we need to understand the concept of logos. It can be in a text form, or a visual or can be a combination of both. It is used to serve two purposes:



1. It tells the people the name of your company

2. It creates a graphic symbol or representation that represents your company.


Some logos have an influential symbolic association that is directly connected and memorized under people’s memory. Like we all have seen Domino’s or McDonald’s logo. The golden arches of the MC Donald are so famous and familiar that they no longer required the text MC Donald under their symbol.

Now coming to next important question, why do you need a logo?


1. It represents your company and mostly tells a lot about your company.

2. A memorable and inspiring logo can evoke and attract potential customers could be interested in doing business with you or even remember you at time of need.

3. A well-designed company logo can deliver everything from the company’s background (professional, relaxed, fun) to its objective (entertainment, efficiency, and innovation) through the right icon or proper font on its logo.

4. It builds an image or a brand that is greater than your identity. 5. Logo Designing is the most crucial. Depending on your design philosophy, simple logos are comprised of only essential elements. They are the most challenging and also successful.


The Most Important Question, How Logo Branding Impact the Company?

Honestly, no one remembers these company’s founders. They both were founded more than 100 years ago, and their founders have been long gone. However, everyone is still using their products and services, everyone remembers them, and thus that is the true power of a logo.


The last but not least, how can you make it?

Knowing the uses and concept of logo is easy but making a real one can be a little frustrating. Choosing and selecting a logo is tricky because once selected, there is no going back. Once it is out in the market, it will be your business identity. No matter what you do, people are going to remember your brand by your logo only. So, it is essential to make sure your logo is adequate to carry out your brand name in the market. To make an appropriate logo, you should consider the following:


1. The text of your logo should be super clear and readable: - When you are starting with your logo, it is essential that your logo text should be clear and must be read at your distance. It is crucial because, during the advertising, it will appear on vehicle signage, building signage, posters, and billboards and would be seen from a distance.

2. Simple colours are usable everywhere: - Your logo might appear on screens, business cards, letterheads, vehicles, shop signage, product packaging, newspaper adverts, and many more. Logos with gradient colours, lots of fine detail, different colours, or photographic content that are much more likely to look quite different in these different situations as many of them use completely different printing technology. It makes it challenging to match the colours exactly. That is why the most influential brands in the world usually stick to a simple palette of less than three primary colours. They also use solid colours rather than gradients.

3. Have something memorable/visual- Like I said from the examples of Coca-Cola and McDonald’s that your company will live on the strength of the logo after you. Some businesses do this by using exciting text or modifying part of the logo, not just to be a standard font. Other companies do this by adding some illustrated icons alongside the text part of the logo. One beneficial aspect of having an icon is that you can use the icon on its own in some situations. For example, the appearance of Nike's tick with the Nike text or without. While initially, the Nike tick would have meant nothing if it didn't appear alongside the Nike text, visual memory association has developed over time.
