Cloud Computingy




Have you ever imagined what made Applications like Google, YouTube, Amazon Prime, Netflix possible?

What is that specific thing through which we can rule over the internet these days?

What allows us to stream on YouTube, watching a show on OTT platforms, allow us to store GBs of data in our Google Drive and send it to anyone via the internet.

Well, we can't say for everyone, but it's a bit complicated to understand; it is called Cloud Computing.

Still, wondering what are we talking about? Technically speaking, "it is the on-demand availability of computer system resources, especially data storage and computing power, without active linear management by the user." But technical language is not everyone's cup of tea, so today, we will learn what cloud computing is and how it changes the IT industry forever.



What is Cloud Computing?



As mentioned above, the technical definition of cloud computing, many people still face the dilemma of understanding it. So, in layman's terms, it means storing and accessing data and programs over the internet instead of your computer's hard drive. All activities like email, banking media streaming and e-commerce are possible due to cloud computing.

How has it been helpful to us?

Now we all know what cloud computing is but the natural thing to understand why it is essential?

And most notably, how has it revolutionized the market?

Cloud computing offers a variety of benefits that have aided the information technology market's growth and development into what it is now. The following are a few of them:

  • Data Analytics-

    • It is the top leverage given by cloud computing to customers by harnessing the benefit of extracting business value. It helps the retailers and suppliers to extract information reaped from consumers' buying patterns to target their advertising and marketing campaigns to a particular segment of the population.


  • Cloud Storage-

    • Offering the possibility of storing your files and accessing, storing, and retrieving them from any web-enabled interface at any time and any place is a game-changing aspect. Today organizations are only paying for the amount of cloud storage they are consuming and do so without overseeing the daily maintenance of the storage infrastructure.


  • Data Backup-

    • - Cloud-based backup, while not being the panacea, is undoubtedly a far cry from what it used to be. It helps to automatically dispatch data to any location across the wire to ensure neither security, availability, nor capacity issues.


  • Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS)-

    • It is a service offering a merchant that provides users access to computing resources such as storage, networking, and servers. Amazon Web Services (AWS) Microsoft Azure. Google Cloud Platform (GCP) Alibaba Cloud are some of the vendors of this service.

  • Platform as a Service (PaaS)-

    • It is a cloud computing model where a third-party provider accouches hardware and software tools to users over the internet. Usually, these tools are needed for application development. AWS Elastic Beanstalk, Windows Azure, Heroku, Force.com, Google App Engine are some of the vendors of this service.



How much has Cloud Computing progressed over the years? Is it a money-making venture?

Cloud computing first came into the limelight in 2006 when the CEO of Google, Eric Schmidt, first introduced this term at the industry conference. Since then, cloud computing has achieved its growth and has never looked back. We can see how much IT development is dependent on the Cloud below:

All computers, so to speak, exist on the Cloud. Most of the emerging technologies like AI, Blockchain, IoT cannot exist without the Cloud. There will be no home working. So, in short, no cloud, no future in technology.


The global Cloud computing market scope is expected to grow from USD 371.4 billion in 2020 to USD 832.1 billion by 2025, at a Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) of 17.5% during the projection period. The demand for cloud computing is rising every year. According to analytical agency Gartner, the global market for general cloud services will reach $ 308.5 billion in 2021. it is $ 40 billion more than in 2020 and 90 billion more than in 2019.

All in all, cloud computing is a new technological development that has the potential to have a significant impact on the world. Cloud computing is the network power, and it holds a lot for us in the future of IT.
