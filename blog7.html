What is blockchain?



In simpler terms, it is the data stored in blocks of information and then linked together in a permanent "chain." It relies on hashes and cryptography to secure the data inside a block. This chain of blocks resides on all computers in a peer-to-peer network.

In a nutshell, blockchain contains a chain of blocks. Every block includes tons of information (record of data, its transaction, and the timestamp).



Who founded it and why?

Peter Smith, Ben Reeves, and Nicolas Cary, the founders of this massive wallet of information, in 2008. Their intention behind this creation was primarily to serve a public transaction ledger of the cryptocurrency like Bitcoin

The goal is to allow digital information to record and distributed but not edited.

Now we know the intentions behind these geniuses; let see how their invention works.


How does blockchain work?


Blockchain never stores its information in a prime location. Instead, the blockchain copies the data and spread it across the network of computers. The more blocks that are added to the blockchain, the more frequently the network is updated.


In step-by-step method:

Step 1- Some person requests a transaction. The transaction could be involved cryptocurrency, contracts, records, or other information.

Step 2- The requested transaction is broadcasted to a P2P network with the help of nodes.

Step 3- The network of nodes validates the transaction and the user's status with the help of known algorithms.

Step 4- Once the transaction is complete, the new block is then added to the existing blockchain so that it is permanent and unalterable.


Is there more than one blockchain?

We already know that blockchain is similar to a ledger. But every ledger is different; some are private, some are public, and some are both like:


1) Public Blockchain- - It is a non-restrictive, permission-less distributed ledger system. A user has access to current and past records, verify transactions or does proof-of-work for an incoming block, and does mining.

Example- Bitcoin, Litecoin, Ethereum.

2) Private Blockchain- - It is a restrictive or permission blockchain operative only in a closed network. The level of security, authorizations, approvals, accessibility is in the hands of the controlling organization. These networks are deployed for voting, supply chain management, digital identity, asset ownership, etc.

Example- Multichain and Hyperledger project

3) Consortium Blockchain- - A consortium blockchain is a semi-decentralized type where more than one organization manages a blockchain network. In this type of blockchain, organizations exchange information or do mining. Banks, government organizations are usually the users.

Example- Energy Web Foundation, R3, etc.

4) Hybrid Blockchain- - A hybrid blockchain is a combination of the private and public blockchain.

Example- Dragon chain.


How is it effective than others?


Now we know what blockchain is and how it works. But the question is why people should pursue it? There are many substitutes for it in the market like Hash graph, R3 Corda, Iota Tangle, etc. So, why should we devote our time and resources to blockchain technology?

1) Reduce the cost of overall transactions.

2) Reduction in systematic risks.

3) Irrevocable and tamper-resistant transactions.

4) Fraud minimalization

5) Improved security and efficiency of transactions.

6) Enabling effective monitoring and auditing by participants, supervisors, and regulators.
